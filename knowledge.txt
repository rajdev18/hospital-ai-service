Cardiologist treats heart diseases, chest pain, high blood pressure, and heart attacks.
Neurologist treats persistent headaches, migraines, seizures, stroke, and brain disorders.
Dermatologist treats skin diseases, rashes, acne, eczema, and skin infections.
Orthopedic treats bone fractures, joint pain, arthritis, and sports injuries.
Gastroenterologist treats stomach pain, digestive issues, ulcers, and liver diseases.
Psychiatrist treats depression, anxiety, mental health disorders, and stress.
Pediatrician treats diseases and health issues in children and infants.
Ophthalmologist treats eye diseases, vision problems, and eye infections.
ENT specialist treats ear, nose, and throat infections and disorders.
Pulmonologist treats lung diseases, asthma, breathing problems, and tuberculosis.
General Physician treats common illnesses like fever, cold, cough, body pain, and general health issues.
General Physician should be consulted first for fever, body ache, weakness, and fatigue.
General Physician treats viral infections, bacterial infections, and common diseases.
Chest pain and shortness of breath should be seen by a Cardiologist.
Persistent severe headache without fever should be seen by a Neurologist.
Severe headache with high fever may indicate infection and should be seen by a General Physician first.
Skin rash and itching should be seen by a Dermatologist.
Joint pain and swelling should be seen by an Orthopedic.
Stomach pain and nausea should be seen by a Gastroenterologist.
Anxiety and depression should be seen by a Psychiatrist.
Fever and cold in children should be seen by a Pediatrician.
Blurred vision and eye pain should be seen by an Ophthalmologist.
Ear pain and throat infection should be seen by an ENT specialist.
Cough and breathing difficulty should be seen by a Pulmonologist.
Fever is a common symptom caused by infection, inflammation, or other medical conditions.
Common cold is a viral infection causing runny nose, sore throat, and mild fever.
Diabetes is a condition where blood sugar levels are too high.
Hypertension is high blood pressure that increases risk of heart disease and stroke.
Asthma is a chronic lung disease causing difficulty breathing and wheezing.
Allergies occur when immune system reacts to foreign substances like pollen or food.
Back pain can be caused by muscle strain, poor posture, or spinal problems.
Fatigue and tiredness can be caused by anemia, thyroid problems, or lack of sleep.
Weight loss or weight gain can indicate thyroid disorders or diabetes.
Frequent urination and excessive thirst are symptoms of diabetes.
Chest tightness and wheezing are symptoms of asthma or lung disease.
Numbness and tingling in hands or feet can indicate nerve damage or diabetes.
Memory loss and confusion in elderly can indicate dementia or Alzheimer's disease.
High fever with stiff neck can indicate meningitis and needs emergency care.
Sudden severe chest pain can indicate heart attack and needs emergency care.
Difficulty speaking or sudden weakness can indicate stroke and needs emergency care.
Blood in urine can indicate kidney stones or urinary tract infection.
Painful urination and frequent urination indicate urinary tract infection.
Swollen lymph nodes can indicate infection or in rare cases lymphoma.
Yellow skin and eyes indicate jaundice which can be caused by liver disease.
Fever with body ache and weakness should be seen by a General Physician.
Mild headache with fever should be seen by a General Physician.
Runny nose, sore throat and mild fever should be seen by a General Physician.
